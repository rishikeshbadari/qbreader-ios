QBReader Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 18, 2026

QBReader is a lightweight iOS app for quiz bowl tossup practice and multiplayer games. This policy explains what information the app handles and how it is used.

Information We Collect

QBReader does not require an account and does not collect personal information for advertising or tracking.

The app may handle the following information:

  • Local gameplay data: single-player history, answers, settings, and preferences are stored locally on your device.
  • Multiplayer gameplay data: when you join or host a multiplayer game, the app transmits room codes, player names, answers, scores, and game state needed to run the room.
  • Support emails: if you contact support by email, your email address and message are received so we can respond.

How Information Is Used

Information is used only to provide app functionality, including loading questions, checking answers, saving local history, syncing multiplayer rooms, and responding to support requests.

Third-Party Services

QBReader uses the QB Reader API to load quiz bowl questions and Supabase Realtime to support multiplayer rooms. These services may process technical information necessary to deliver network requests and realtime game state.

Tracking and Advertising

QBReader does not show ads, does not use third-party advertising SDKs, and does not track users across apps or websites.

Data Retention

Single-player history and settings remain on your device unless you delete the app or clear app data. Multiplayer room data is transient and used to run active games. Support emails may be retained as needed to respond to your request.

Children

QBReader is intended for general quiz bowl and trivia practice. The app does not knowingly collect personal information from children for tracking or advertising.

Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated as the app changes. The effective date above will be updated when material changes are made.
